Fermented rice

Pakhala-A Traditioanl Odisa recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked rice (preferably leftover rice from the previous day)
  • 1.5 to 2 cups of water (room temperature or boiled and cooled)
  • A pinch of salt
  • crushed garlic cloves (optional, traditional in some regions)
  • A few curry leaves or green chili (optional, for flavor)
  • Mustard seeds or a dash of mustard oil (optional, regional variation)

Steps

  1. Cook the rice as you normally would, let it cool completely.
  2. Transfer the rice to a bowl or clay pot.
  3. Add water to fully submerge the rice. The water should be at room temperature or cooled boiled water.
  4. Cover the bowl loosely (not airtight) and leave it at room temperature overnight or for 12–24 hours.
  5. In hotter climates, 8–10 hours may be enough.
  6. The next morning, it will have a slightly sour smell – a sign of fermentation.
  7. To serve, you can:
    • Eat it plain with salt.
    • Add chopped onions, green chilies, mustard oil, and curd/yogurt.
    • Pair it with fried fish, pickle, or sautéed vegetables for a complete meal.
